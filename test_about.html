Research Interests

My research interests include data privacy and security, privacy-preserving AI, and the security of large language models (LLMs) and AI systems.

  • Data Privacy and Security We design differential privacy (DP) mechanisms for a wide range of data analytics tasks and realistic application settings, aiming to safeguard data privacy in analytical outcomes. Our focus is on developing mechanisms that are both theoretically sound and empirically effective.
  • Privacy-Preserving AI We leverage privacy-enhancing technologies such as differential privacy (DP), secure multi-party computation (MPC), and zero-knowledge proofs (ZKP) to protect data privacy across the entire AI lifecycle—from data collection and training to deployment and inference.
  • LLM and AI Security We explore the security and misuse challenges associated with large language models (LLMs) and AI systems, with emphasis on designing empirical methods to preserve data privacy in LLM and AI applications; protecting intellectual property (IP) in LLMs; defending against malicious uses of LLMs; and utilizing LLMs to enhance the security of existing systems.
